ISO
Sensor sensitivity. ISO 100 — clean image, needs lots of light. ISO 6400 — brighter, but noise appears.
Aperture
Lens opening size. ƒ/1.4 — wide open, blurred background (bokeh). ƒ/16 — closed, everything sharp.
Shutter Speed
Time the shutter stays open. 1/1000 — freezes motion. 1/30 — motion blur, use a tripod.
Triangle
All three parameters control exposure together. Change one — compensate with another.
ISO ↑ = noise ↑ ƒ/ ↓ = bokeh ↑ 1/t ↓ = blur ↑

Glossary
ISO
Light Sensitivity▾
ISO 100 — clean image, needs lots of light. ISO 6400 — shoot in the dark, but grain appears.
ISO 100 — outdoorsISO 800 — indoorsISO 6400 — night
Aperture
Aperture · ƒ-number▾
Small ƒ-number (ƒ/1.4) — wide opening, more light, blurred background. Large (ƒ/16) — everything in focus.
ƒ/1.4 — bokehƒ/5.6 — versatileƒ/16 — landscape
Shutter Speed
Shutter Speed▾
1/1000s — freezes motion. 1/30s — motion blurs, use a tripod.
1/1000 — sport1/60 — portrait1/8 — blur
Exposure
Exposure · EV▾
Total light hitting the sensor. EV 0 = normal, +2 = overexposed, −2 = too dark.
0 EV — ideal+2 EV — overexposed−2 EV — too dark
Bokeh
Bokeh · 暈け▾
The aesthetic of out-of-focus blur. Achieved with a wide aperture. Character depends on the lens.
ƒ/1.4 — strongHelios — swirlyLeica — dreamy
DOF
Depth of Field▾
The zone where objects appear sharp. Shallow DOF (ƒ/1.4) — only the subject is sharp. Deep DOF (ƒ/16) — everything sharp.
Shallow → portraitDeep → landscape
Exposure Triangle
Exposure Triangle▾
The relationship between ISO, aperture, and shutter speed. Changing one requires compensating with another — the basis of manual mode.
ISO ↑ → noise ↑ƒ ↓ → bokeh ↑1/t ↓ → blur ↑
